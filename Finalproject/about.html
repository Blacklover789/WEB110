How Esports Began

Competitive gaming traces back to arcade competitions and early LAN tournaments. Over the last two decades, esports has grown into a global industry with professional leagues, sponsorships, and large prize pools.

Streaming platforms like Twitch and YouTube made it possible for fans to watch live events and follow professional players. This helped build large communities and commercial interest.

Today, universities offer esports scholarships and major brands sponsor teams — a sign of esports' increasing legitimacy.
